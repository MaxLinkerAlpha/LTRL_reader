## Prepositions
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>介词 <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='latin-text'>A preposition (praepono,place before)is a word placed before a noun or pronoun to show its relation to another word in the sentence.</div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>介词 (praepono,置于前)是一类置于名词或代词之前，用于表达其与句中其余成分间关系的词。 <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='Yancey' style='border-color:#FDEBFF'>介词 (praepositio，源自praepono，意为“前置”)是一种位于名词或代词前，表达自身与其他句子成分关系的词类。 <span class='tag' style='background:#FDEBFF'>Yancey</span></div>
<div class='latin-text'>The preposition and the noun or pronoun together are called a "prepositional phrase."In Latin,prepositions are most often followed by one of two cases,the accusative or the ablative.</div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>介词与名词或代词一同构成介词短语。在拉丁语中，介词后的单词绝大多数情况下是宾格或离格。 <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='latin-text'>Prepositions that require a noun in the accusative case are said to"take the accusative"and are marked in the vocabulary entry by the notation (prep.+acc.).</div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>若一个介词的后置单词为宾格，那么我们说这个介词“取宾格”，在单词表中标记为 ？（介+宾） <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='Yancey' style='border-color:#FDEBFF'>若一个介词的后置单词为宾格，那么我们说这个介词“*后接宾格”，在单词表中标记为(praep.+acc.)（即praepositio+accusativus (casus)） <span class='tag' style='background:#FDEBFF'>Yancey</span></div>
<div class='latin-text'>Prepositions that take the accusative emphasize the idea of motion toward,into,around,and through.</div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>取宾格的介词用于强调“前往”“进入”“环绕”“通过”这些概念 <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='latin-text'>Prepositions that require a noun in the ablative case are simi- larly said to"take the ablative"and are marked by the notation (prep.+abl.).</div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>类似的，若一个介词的后置单词为离格，那么我们说这个介词“取离格”，标记为（介+离） <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='Yancey' style='border-color:#FDEBFF'>类似的，若一个介词的后置单词为离格，那么我们说这个介词“*后跟离格”，标记为(praep.+abl.)（即praepositio+ablativus (casus)） <span class='tag' style='background:#FDEBFF'>Yancey</span></div>
<div class='latin-text'>Prepositions that take the ablative indicate one of the three functions of the ablative(separation,association/ instrument,location).</div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>取离格的介词则暗含着离格的三个功能（表分离、表连携或表手段、表位置）之一。 <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='latin-text'>A few prepositions can take either case,and their meanings differ according to which case they take.</div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>一些介词既可以取宾格也可以取离格，它们的含义也随之改变。 <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='Yancey' style='border-color:#FDEBFF'>一般而言，若动词表示动作，则介词后接宾格；若动词表示静止，则介词后跟夺格。 <span class='tag' style='background:#FDEBFF'>Yancey</span></div>

<div class='latin-text'>&gt; et is a coordinating conjunction. This means that it connects only parallel or grammatically balanced words, phrases, or clauses. When two nouns are connected, they must be in the same case: for example, nautārum et agricolarum (of the sailors and of the farmers (genitive]).</div>
<div class='translation' data-translator='tuche est' style='border-color:#00A3F5'>et是个并列连词，它仅用于连接同类或语法上一致的单词、短语、或从句。它连接的两个的名词需同格：例如，nautārum et agricolarum（水手们的与农民们的） <span class='tag' style='background:#00A3F5'>tuche est</span></div>
<div class='latin-text'>Parts of speech other than nouns may also be connected by et. For example, in the phrases "tall and snow-covered," "he sings and he dances," "in Italy and in Gaul," et could again be used to connect two adjectives, two verb phrases, or two prepositional phrases.</div>

## 派生词和同源词
<div class='latin-text'>Many words in English are derived from Latin words; that is, they descend directly from words in Latin. Such words are called derivatives. For example, the English word "counsel" is a derivative of the Latin word cônsilium.A</div>

<div class='latin-text'>In many instances, the differences in sound and spelling between an English word and its Latin parent are not significant enough to obscure the fact that the two words are related.</div>
